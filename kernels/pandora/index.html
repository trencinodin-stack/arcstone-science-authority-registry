Existential Risk Containment Doctrine

Governance Doctrine

Defines conditions under which irreversible action must be suspended due to existential or systemic hazard.

Purpose

This doctrine establishes a conservative posture for high-stakes uncertainty: when hazard indicators exceed admissible thresholds, the correct classification outcome is suspension pending improved evidence, reversibility, or oversight. It describes a safety stance without specifying mechanisms, detection methods, or operational triggers.

Classification States

  • Normal
  • Elevated Risk
  • High Risk
  • Suspended

Canonical public registry entry. Descriptive and non-operational.
