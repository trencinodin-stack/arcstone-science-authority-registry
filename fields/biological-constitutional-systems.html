Biological Constitutional Systems

Study of constitutional constraints governing biological states and actions.

Core Concerns

  • Admissibility of biological states
  • Constraint-governed biological transitions
  • Fail-closed biological safety boundaries
  • Irreversible biological risk
  • Separation of governance from diagnosis

Scope

Biological Constitutional Systems concern the governance of what biological states and interventions are permitted to occur, independent of clinical interpretation or therapeutic intent. The field prioritizes safety, legality, and irreversibility constraints over optimization or outcome prediction.
