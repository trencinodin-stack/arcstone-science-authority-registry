Constitutional Mathematics

Study of mathematical systems in which constraints, legality, and admissibility are primary objects.

Core Concerns

  • Constraint-first mathematical formulation
  • Admissible state and transition spaces
  • Proof-governed legality
  • Fail-closed mathematical systems
  • Non-permissible solutions and collapse conditions

Scope

Constitutional Mathematics governs mathematical frameworks where not all solutions are allowed to exist. It prioritizes legality, constraint satisfaction, and admissibility over optimization, expressiveness, or completeness.
