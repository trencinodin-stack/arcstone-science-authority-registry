Authorization-Centric Substrate Science (ACSS)

Study of mathematical and physical substrates in which existence is conditional on authorization.

Core Concerns

  • Substrate-level admissibility constraints
  • Permission as a physical and mathematical property
  • Existence conditional on lawful state space
  • Non-executable states and transitions
  • Fail-closed substrate architectures

Scope

Authorization-Centric Substrate Science concerns the foundations beneath systems, where authorization is not a policy choice but a structural condition. It examines substrates in which states, transitions, or entities cannot exist unless permitted by underlying mathematical or physical constraints.
