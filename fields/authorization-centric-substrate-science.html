Authorization-Centric Substrate Science (ACSS)

Study of mathematical and physical substrates in which existence is conditional on authorization.

Core Concerns

  • Substrate-level admissibility constraints
  • Permission as a structural property
  • Existence conditional on lawful state space
  • Non-executable states and transitions
  • Fail-closed substrate architectures

Scope

Authorization-Centric Substrate Science concerns foundational substrates where authorization is not a policy decision but a structural requirement. It examines systems in which states, entities, or transitions cannot exist unless permitted by underlying mathematical or physical constraints.
